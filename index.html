Hate Crimes Hit New Highs

Statistical analysis underlines the hate for Jews in New York

By Jake Lang | (@808sJake)

Date: December 17th, 2023

The rise in anti-Jewish hate crimes and incidents in New York City, particularly following the events of October 7th, is raising significant concerns. According to the official NYPD Hate Crimes Dashboard 131 of 197 across October and November incidents were anti-Jewish, with fifteen anti-Muslim hate crimes being the second most out of an overal 124% increase in hate crimes overall according to NY1.

At the same, it's important to remember to note that hate crimes, like with incidents of sexual violence, reporting can be complicated.

“Many victims of hate crimes are reluctant to report them to the police. And reported crimes do not always lead to arrests, prosecutions or even a record of hate crimes.” - The Center For Public Integrity

An analysis of publically available data provided ny the New York Division of Criminal Justice Services additionally reveals that between 2010 and 2022, approximately 40.44% of all hate crimes across the state and 44.75% in NYC have been classified as anti-Jewish. It's clear with statistics like this, the seriousness of the issue and the need for continued efforts to combat anti-Semitism and all hate crimes in New York City and beyond.
